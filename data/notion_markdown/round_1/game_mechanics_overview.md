# Game Mechanics Overview

## Rounds

The 16 days of simulation of Prosperity are divided into 5 rounds. Rounds 1 and 2 last 72 hours, while rounds 3, 4, and 5 each last 48 hours.

At the end of every round - before the timer runs out - all teams will have to submit their algorithmic and manual trades to be processed. The algorithms will then participate in a full day of trading against the Prosperity trading bots. Note that all algorithms are trading separately, there is no interaction between the algorithms of different players. When a new round starts, the results of the previous round will be disclosed and the leaderboard will be updated accordingly. During the game, you can always visit previous rounds in the dashboard, to review information and results. But once a round is closed, you can no longer change your submitted trader for that round. When Round 5 ends, the final results will be processed and the winner of the Prosperity trading challenge will be announced within 2 weeks.

### Algorithmic trading

Every round contains an algorithmic trading challenge. You will have to submit your (final) Python program before the trading round ends. When the round ends, the last successfully processed submission will be locked in and processed for results.

### Manual trading

Every round also contains a manual trading challenge taking place at the same time. Similar to the algorithmic trading rounds, the last submission will be locked in and processed and executed at the end of the round. During the tutorial round, manual trading is inactive. Manual trades have no effect on your algorithmic trade and can be seen as separate challenges to gain additional profits.

## Dashboard

### Algorithmic trading submissions

Submitting your algorithmic trading program (your Python program) is done through the Mission Control dashboard, by clicking the "Challenge Details" button under the Algorithmic Challenge.

An Algorithmic Challenge Overview window contains essential information to build your Python program, including the Data Capsule with historical trade data for available tradable goods.

Clicking the "Upload Algorithm" button opens the Upload and Changelog window where you can upload your file and view previously uploaded programs with status information.

You and your team can upload as many Python programs as you like, but only the active algorithm will be processed and executed at the end of the round.

### Manual trading submissions

Submitting your manual trades is done through the Manual Challenge Overview window.

You and your team can resubmit strategies as many times as you like, but only the last submitted trade will be processed and executed at the end of the round.

## A.R.I.A. Uplinks

A.R.I.A. Uplinks are a primary source of information for Prosperity. A new Uplink is available at the beginning of every round and provides essential information for algorithmic and manual trading activities.

## Outpost View

The Outpost View shows your team name, PnL indicator, rank, and links to the leaderboard and crew honors. Mission Control is available from this view.

## Leaderboard

The leaderboard includes Overall, Algorithmic, Manual, and Country ranking views.

## Crew Honors

While you play Prosperity, you can earn badges for actions and achievements, and view them on the Crew Honors page.

## On-Board Advisors

Teams can choose one of three advisors during the Tutorial Round. The choice is locked when the Tutorial Round ends, and advisors provide guidance from Round 1 onward.
